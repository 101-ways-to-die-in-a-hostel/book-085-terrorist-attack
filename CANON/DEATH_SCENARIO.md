# Death Scenario

## The Death (Summary)
**Method:** Killed instantly by shrapnel from a bomb detonated in a restaurant
**Category:** Circumstantial
**Duration:** Instant
**Witnesses:** None survive from his immediate vicinity; survivors from periphery

## Mechanism (Technical)

### The Cause
Massive trauma from explosion and shrapnel. Stefan was seated near the blast center. Death was instantaneous - he never knew what happened.

### The Setup
- Stefan had a reservation at an expensive tourist restaurant for his "splurge" night
- That morning, he saw the prices again and calculated he could save 20 euros elsewhere
- Canceled reservation and chose a cheaper local restaurant instead
- The "cheap" restaurant was targeted by extremists specifically because tourists avoided it
- The tourist restaurant was untouched

### The Trigger
The bomb was on a timer, set by an attacker who left 30 minutes before. Stefan happened to arrive 10 minutes before detonation. He was seated, enjoying bread, when the device exploded.

### The Process

1. Stefan arrives at the restaurant, happy about his choice
2. Seated near the window (best view, he thought)
3. Orders economical meal, delighted by prices
4. Bomb detonates under a nearby table
5. Instant death - no awareness, no suffering

## Research Notes
- Restaurant bombings have unfortunately occurred in tourist areas worldwide
- Shrapnel injuries are the primary cause of death in such attacks
- Immediate proximity to blast center typically results in instant death
- Survivors' guilt and PTSD affect witnesses for decades

## The Death Sequence (Full Detail)

### Setup (Chapter 35-37)
Stefan has had his best day of travel yet. Beautiful weather, amazing sights. He's decided tonight is his "special" dinner. He checks the tourist restaurant price again. Too much. He finds an alternative.

### Trigger (Chapter 38)
He walks past the expensive restaurant (with its security screening), and heads to the cheaper spot. He's delighted. The irony is invisible to him.

### The Death (Chapters 39-40)
Write this sequence in full detail:

**7:15 PM:**
Stefan enters the restaurant. Quiet place, few patrons. A waiter seats him by the window with a view of the square. He smiles.

**7:20 PM:**
He orders the daily special - half the price of the tourist menu. Bread arrives. It's excellent. He writes a note in his journal about the meal.

**7:25 PM:**
A nervous man in the corner finishes his drink and leaves. Stefan doesn't notice.

**7:28 PM:**
Stefan asks the waiter for more bread. The waiter smiles and turns toward the kitchen.

**7:29 PM:**
Blinding light. Sound beyond sound. Everything ends.

**The world continues:**
The tourist restaurant two blocks away is fine. Stefan's reservation there goes unused. His mother will get a phone call in 17 hours.

### Immediate Aftermath
23 dead, 47 wounded. Stefan's body is identified by his passport in his money belt. His journal survives, last entry mid-sentence. His mother collapses when Polish officials arrive at her door.

## Sensory Details

### Sight
Stefan sees: warm restaurant light, the square through the window, good bread, the waiter's back as he walks to the kitchen. Then nothing.

### Sound
Conversation murmur, distant traffic, his own voice asking for bread. Then a sound that isn't a sound - pure force.

### Touch/Pain
Nothing. No pain. The mercy of instant death.

### Smell
Fresh bread. Cooking food. Then nothing.

### Taste
Good bread, butter. The last taste is satisfaction.

## The Horror Element
The pure randomness. The cruel irony that his frugality - which saved for years to make this trip possible - killed him. The alternate universe where he spent 20 more euros and lived.

## The Comedy Element
There is almost none. The darkest possible irony: the tourist trap was the safe space. His carefully maintained budget spreadsheet is found, showing he died with 847 euros remaining in his trip fund.

### Pacing the Death
Leisurely build of a good day, emphasizing Stefan's simple pleasures. The death is abrupt - mid-sentence, mid-meal, mid-life. No warning, no build-up.

### POV During Death
